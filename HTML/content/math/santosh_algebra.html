Profit and Loss in Math

Profit and Loss are basic financial concepts used in business and everyday transactions. They help determine whether a business or individual gains or loses money in a transaction.

Profit

When the selling price (SP) of an item is greater than its cost price (CP), the seller makes a profit.

Formula:

Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price

Profit Percentage:

Profit% = (Profit / Cost Price) ├Ч 100

Loss

When the selling price (SP) of an item is less than its cost price (CP), the seller incurs a loss.

Formula:

Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price

Loss Percentage:

Loss% = (Loss / Cost Price) ├Ч 100

Selling Price and Cost Price Formulas

Selling Price when profit is given:

SP = CP + Profit

Selling Price when loss is given:

SP = CP - Loss
