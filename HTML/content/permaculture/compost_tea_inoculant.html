Complete Guide to Making Compost Tea and Inoculant

Understanding Compost Tea

Compost tea is a liquid extract of compost that contains beneficial microorganisms, nutrients, and organic compounds. When properly brewed, it serves as both a fertilizer and a powerful inoculant that can enhance soil biology and plant health. There are two main types of compost tea:

  • Aerated Compost Tea (ACT): Brewed with active aeration to maintain high oxygen levels
  • Non-aerated Compost Tea: Steeped without additional aeration
Why Use Compost Tea?
  • Introduces beneficial microorganisms to soil
  • Improves nutrient cycling and availability
  • Enhances plant disease resistance
  • Increases soil organic matter
  • Promotes healthier root systems

Essential Equipment

For Aerated Compost Tea:

  • 5-gallon bucket (food-grade)
  • Air pump (aquarium style)
  • Air stones or diffusers
  • Food-grade tubing
  • Fine mesh bag or paint strainer
  • Thermometer
  • pH test strips

For Non-aerated Tea:

  • 5-gallon bucket (food-grade)
  • Fine mesh bag or paint strainer
  • Stirring stick (wooden)

Base Ingredients

Basic Recipe (5-gallon batch):

  • 2-3 cups high-quality finished compost
  • 4.5 gallons of dechlorinated water
  • 1/4 cup unsulfured molasses
  • 1/4 cup fish hydrolysate
  • 1/4 cup kelp meal
Important: Use only fully finished, mature compost. Immature compost can contain pathogens and phytotoxic compounds.

Making Aerated Compost Tea

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Prepare the Water (Day 1)
    • Fill bucket with dechlorinated water
    • Ensure water temperature is between 65-75°F (18-24°C)
    • Set up air pump and stones
    • Begin aeration and run for 20 minutes
  2. Add Ingredients (Day 1)
    • Place compost in mesh bag
    • Suspend bag in water
    • Add molasses, fish hydrolysate, and kelp meal
    • Ensure strong aeration continues
  3. Brewing Period (24-36 hours)
    • Maintain constant aeration
    • Keep temperature stable
    • Check for sweet, earthy smell
  4. Straining and Storage
    • Remove compost bag
    • Strain if necessary
    • Use immediately for best results
Quality Indicators:
  • Sweet, earthy smell
  • Light to medium brown color
  • Small bubbles on surface
  • No foul odors

Making Non-aerated Compost Tea

Simple Steeping Method:

  1. Initial Setup
    • Fill bucket with dechlorinated water
    • Place compost in mesh bag
    • Add bag to water
  2. Steeping Process
    • Steep for 7-14 days
    • Stir daily
    • Keep in shaded area
  3. Completion
    • Remove bag
    • Strain if needed
    • Use within 24 hours

Creating Specialized Inoculants

Fungal-Dominant Tea

Additional Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup humic acid
  • 1 cup oatmeal (finely ground)
  • 1/4 cup powdered kelp

Bacterial-Dominant Tea

Additional Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup fish hydrolysate
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 tablespoon rock dust

Application Methods

Method Dilution Frequency Best For
Foliar Spray 1:10 Weekly Leaf health, disease prevention
Soil Drench 1:5 Monthly Root development, soil biology
Seed Soak 1:20 Pre-planting Germination, early growth

Troubleshooting

Common Issues:
Problem Likely Cause Solution
Foul smell Anaerobic conditions Increase aeration, reduce nutrients
No bubbles Poor aeration Check pump, clean air stones
Thick scum layer Too many nutrients Reduce molasses/fish hydrolysate

Storage and Shelf Life

Compost tea is a living product and should be used as soon as possible after brewing. However, if storage is necessary:

Storage Guidelines:
  • Use within 24 hours for best results
  • Store in a cool, shaded area
  • Keep container loosely covered
  • Continue aeration if storing ACT
  • Check for off-odors before use
