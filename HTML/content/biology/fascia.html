Understanding the Myofascial System

Glossary

Fascia
The biological container and connector tissue that forms a continuous web throughout the body.
ECM (Extra-cellular Matrix)
Everything in your body that isn't cellular, including fibers, glue, and water.
Thixotropic
A property where tissue becomes more fluid when moved and stiffer when still.
Proprioception
The body's ability to sense its position and movement in space.
Fourth Phase of Water
A liquid crystalline phase of water that forms near hydrophilic surfaces, having ordered molecular structure.
Piezoelectricity
The ability of certain materials to generate electrical charge in response to mechanical stress.
Craniosacral Rhythm
The rhythmic flow of cerebrospinal fluid and associated tissue movement monitored in craniosacral therapy.
Sol-Gel State
The transition of fascia between fluid (sol) and more solid (gel) states in response to pressure and movement.

Myofascial Release and Tissue Response

Emotional Trauma and the Fascial System

The fascial system plays a key role in emotional processing and trauma storage:

  • Emotional components:
    • Fascia stores emotional memory in its matrix
    • The solar plexus area often holds grief and sadness
    • Trauma can create persistent tension patterns
    • Myofascial release may trigger emotional responses during treatment
  • Treatment considerations:
    • Practitioner should maintain verbal contact during emotional releases
    • Multiple sessions may be needed to fully process stored emotions
    • Physical and emotional releases often occur together
    • A safe, supportive environment is essential

When gentle, sustained pressure and vibration are applied to fascia:

  • Piezoelectric effects:
    • Pressure creates electrical charges in collagen fibers
    • These electrical signals influence cellular behavior
    • Tissue begins transitioning from gel to fluid state
  • Tissue response:
    • Fascial restrictions begin to release
    • Increased hydration and fluid movement
    • Changes in tissue tension patterns
    • Release of holding patterns related to past stress
  • Treatment approach:
    • Gentle, sustained pressure (3-5 minutes minimum)
    • Light vibration enhances piezoelectric response
    • Work with tissue's natural response timing
    • Allow for unwinding of tension patterns

The Fascial System

Fascia is an interconnected web of connective tissue containing:

  • Fibers: Strong, pliable weave of collagen and elastin
  • Glue: Variable gels like heparin and hyaluronic acid
  • Water: Fluid that keeps fibers pliable and enables exchange

Pericardial Fascia and Craniosacral Rhythm

The pericardium is a specialized fascial sac that surrounds and protects the heart. It consists of:

  • A tough fibrous outer layer that anchors the heart
  • A smooth serous inner layer allowing frictionless movement
  • A fluid-filled space between layers providing hydraulic shock absorption

In craniosacral therapy, practitioners work with the pericardial pulse, which:

  • Has a distinct rhythm separate from cardiac and respiratory rhythms
  • Reflects the movement of cerebrospinal fluid through the central nervous system
  • Creates subtle pulsations felt throughout the fascial network
  • Typically cycles at 6-12 times per minute
  • Serves as a diagnostic tool for assessing overall fascial health

Piezoelectric Properties

Fascia exhibits piezoelectric properties through its collagen structure. When mechanical force is applied:

  • Collagen fibers generate electrical potentials in response to pressure
  • This creates charge separation within the collagen molecules
  • The electrical signals contribute to:
    • Mechanosensation via Piezo1 and Piezo2 receptors
    • Tissue repair and remodeling processes
    • Pain signal transmission
    • Biomechanical adaptations

Fascial Adaptation

Fascia demonstrates long-term adaptations to mechanical stress and trauma:

  • Molecular reorganization occurs in response to repeated forces
  • Changes in fascial density and organization can persist
  • Mechanical stress triggers biochemical responses including:
    • Altered collagen production
    • Changes in ground substance composition
    • Modified cellular activity in fibroblasts
