Comprehensive Care Guide for Children's Home Staff

HIV Care Protocol

Medication Management

  • Maintain a secure medication storage system with controlled access
  • Keep detailed medication logs for each child
  • Set up medication reminders and establish routines
  • Monitor side effects and document any adverse reactions
  • Ensure backup power supply for medication refrigeration

Medical Monitoring

  • Schedule regular CD4 count and viral load tests
  • Track growth and development metrics
  • Monitor for opportunistic infections
  • Maintain relationships with HIV specialist physicians

Nutrition Requirements

  • Provide high-protein, nutrient-rich meals
  • Ensure adequate caloric intake based on age and condition
  • Monitor for medication-food interactions
  • Supplement with vitamins as prescribed

Weekly Care Schedule

  • Health assessment by facility nurse
  • Weight monitoring and recording
  • Medication inventory check
  • Deep cleaning of living spaces
  • Activity planning for following week
  • Staff rotation schedule updates

Monthly Requirements

  • Comprehensive medical review
  • Medication prescription renewals
  • Growth and development assessment
  • Staff training refresher sessions
  • Emergency protocol review
  • Supply inventory and ordering

Annual Requirements

  • Complete physical examinations
  • Dental checkups
  • Immunization updates
  • Staff certification renewals
  • Facility license renewal
  • Emergency evacuation drills
  • Policy and procedure review

Emergency Protocols

Medical Emergencies

  1. Assess the situation and ensure immediate safety
  2. Contact on-call medical staff
  3. Call emergency services if needed
  4. Administer first aid as appropriate
  5. Contact facility manager
  6. Document the incident

HIV-Specific Emergencies

  • Severe Side Effects: Monitor for and immediately report severe reactions to medications
  • Missed Medications: Follow missed dose protocols as prescribed by physician
  • Opportunistic Infections: Watch for sudden fever, respiratory issues, or other concerning symptoms
  • Exposure Incidents: Follow post-exposure protocols for staff or other children
