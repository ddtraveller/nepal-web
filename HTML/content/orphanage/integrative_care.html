Clinical Guidelines: Integrative Care Protocol for HIV/AIDS Patients

Critical Implementation Notice

This protocol is designed to complement, not replace, standard antiretroviral therapy (ART). All integrative treatments must be implemented under direct supervision with careful monitoring for drug interactions and adverse effects.

Evidence-Based Framework

Current Evidence Summary

Based on systematic review evidence (Cochrane Review), current data supports limited use of specific complementary treatments. Implementation should follow these evidence-based guidelines:

  • Primary treatment must remain conventional ART
  • Complementary treatments should focus on symptom management and quality of life
  • Monitor for interactions with ART medications
  • Document all interventions and outcomes

Clinical Protocols

Metabolic Support Protocol

Implement based on individual patient assessment:

  • GlyNAC supplementation for oxidative stress and metabolic function
  • Regular monitoring of metabolic markers
  • Nutritional counseling and support
  • Documentation of metabolic parameters

Herbal Medicine Guidelines

When implementing herbal treatments:

  • IGM-1 (Chinese herbal formula): May improve quality of life in symptomatic patients
  • SP-303: Consider for chronic diarrhea management
  • Chinese herbal compound SH: Potential adjunct to ART with careful monitoring
  • Document baseline symptoms and track changes

Required monitoring:

  • Liver function tests every 2 weeks initially, then monthly
  • Regular assessment of potential herb-drug interactions
  • Monitor CD4 counts and viral load according to standard protocols

Nutritional Support Protocol

  • Implement high-protein, nutrient-dense diet plans
  • Monitor nutritional status indicators
  • Supplement with approved vitamins and minerals
  • Regular dietary counseling

Monitoring and Documentation Requirements

Required Assessments

Parameter Frequency Documentation
Liver Function Biweekly for first month, then monthly Standard lab form
CD4 Count Every 3 months Immunology report
Viral Load Every 3 months Virology report
Quality of Life Assessment Monthly Standardized QoL form

Adverse Event Management

Required Actions for Adverse Events

  • Immediately document any adverse reactions
  • Report serious adverse events within 24 hours
  • Suspend complementary treatments if adverse events occur
  • Conduct follow-up assessment within 48 hours
  • Complete incident report forms

Last Updated: February 2025

Note: These guidelines should be reviewed and updated quarterly based on new evidence and clinical experience.

Pediatric Nutrition and Activity Guidelines

Dietary Requirements for HIV-Positive Children

Core Nutritional Principles

  • Energy Requirements:
    • Asymptomatic HIV: 10% increase above normal requirements
    • Symptomatic HIV: 20-30% increase above normal requirements
    • During growth spurts: Additional 10% increase
  • Protein Requirements:
    • 12-15% of total calories from protein
    • Focus on complete proteins (eggs, lean meats, legumes with grains)
    • Additional protein during growth phases and infection recovery
  • Essential Nutrients:
    • Iron-rich foods (green leafy vegetables, red meat)
    • Zinc (pumpkin seeds, whole grains)
    • Vitamin A (orange vegetables, eggs)
    • Vitamin C (citrus fruits, bell peppers)
    • B-complex vitamins (whole grains, legumes)

Meal Planning Guidelines

  • Frequency: 5-6 small meals daily
  • Timing: Regular meal schedule coordinated with medications
  • Texture: Appropriate for age and dental status
  • Cultural Considerations: Incorporate familiar local foods

Physical Activity and Development Program

Daily Activity Requirements

  • Structured Physical Activity:
    • Minimum 60 minutes daily of moderate activity
    • Mix of aerobic, strength-building, and flexibility exercises
    • Adapted to individual capacity and health status
  • Age-Appropriate Activities:
    • Ages 3-6: Supervised play, basic motor skills development
    • Ages 7-12: Team sports, dance, swimming
    • Teens: Strength training, endurance activities

Activity Monitoring

  • Regular assessment of:
    • Energy levels
    • Exercise tolerance
    • Recovery time
    • Growth and development markers
  • Activity Modifications During:
    • Acute infections
    • Medication adjustments
    • Growth spurts
    • Recovery periods

Psychosocial Support Integration

  • Group Activities:
    • Peer support groups
    • Supervised team sports
    • Art and music therapy sessions
    • Educational activities
  • Individual Support:
    • Regular counseling sessions
    • Educational support
    • Life skills training
    • Behavioral health monitoring

Special Considerations

  • Medication Timing:
    • Coordinate meals with medication schedule
    • Account for food-drug interactions
    • Monitor for medication side effects affecting nutrition
  • Environmental Factors:
    • Safe food preparation areas
    • Clean water access
    • Proper food storage
    • Hygiene protocols
  • Cultural Integration:
    • Respect for traditional food practices
    • Integration of local dietary customs
    • Cultural activity preferences
    • Community involvement
