Sustainable Farm Plan for 40-Child Orphanage

Livestock Requirements

Dairy and Fish Systems

  • 10-12 Dairy Goats
    • Each goat produces 2-3 liters daily
    • Provide calcium-rich milk
    • Lower maintenance requirements
    • Disease-resistant
    • Can handle varying feed quality
    • Manure excellent for gardens and fish ponds
  • Tilapia Fish Pond System (200-300 fish)
    • High-protein source
    • Fast growth rate (6 months to harvest)
    • Can feed on algae and plant waste
    • Water can be used for garden irrigation
    • Integrated with vegetable growing
  • Catfish Pond System (100-150 fish)
    • Hardy species requiring minimal care
    • High protein content
    • Can handle higher stocking density
    • Good growth rate in varied conditions

Aquaponics Integration

  • 2-3 Tank Systems
    • Growing beds for vegetables above fish tanks
    • Natural filtration system
    • Year-round production
    • Water-efficient system
    • Can grow leafy greens and herbs
  • Required Space:
    • Fish ponds: 200-300 square meters total
    • Aquaponics system: 100 square meters
    • Filtration and processing area: 50 square meters

Poultry

  • 80-100 Laying Hens
    • Provides 60-80 eggs daily
    • High-quality protein source
    • Easy to maintain
    • Quick reproduction cycle
  • 20-30 Meat Chickens
    • Rotating flock for meat production
    • 8-12 week growth cycle
    • Essential protein source
  • 15-20 Ducks
    • Eggs and meat diversity
    • Natural pest control
    • Hardy in varied conditions

Small Livestock

  • 10-12 Rabbits
    • Rapid reproduction
    • Lean meat source
    • Manure for gardens
    • Low feed requirements
  • 2-3 Pigs
    • Efficient food waste converters
    • Large meat yield
    • Can be sold for income

Feed and Space Requirements

Annual Feed Requirements

  • Fish Feed:
    • Tilapia: 400-500 kg commercial feed annually
    • Catfish: 300-400 kg commercial feed annually
    • Supplemental: Duckweed, algae, vegetable scraps
  • Livestock Feed:
    • Grazing Land: 1.5-2 acres for goats
    • Grain Feed: 2-3 tons
    • Hay: 4-5 tons
    • Kitchen Scraps Management System
    • Aquaponics System Feed Integration
  • Integrated Systems:
    • Fish waste for plant fertilization
    • Plant waste for fish and livestock feed
    • Composting system for organic waste
    • Water recycling system

Housing Requirements

  • Goat/Cow Barn: 400-600 sq ft
  • Chicken Coop: 300-400 sq ft
  • Duck House: 150-200 sq ft
  • Rabbit Hutches: 100-150 sq ft
  • Pig Pen: 200-300 sq ft
  • Feed Storage: 300-400 sq ft

Management System

Daily Tasks

  • Fish System Management:
    • Check water temperature (morning/evening)
    • Monitor oxygen levels
    • Feed fish (2-3 times daily)
    • Check filtration systems
    • Remove any dead fish
    • Inspect aquaponics plant health
  • Other Livestock:
    • Morning/Evening Goat Milking
    • Egg Collection (2-3 times daily)
    • Regular Feeding Schedule
    • Health Checks
  • System Integration:
    • Water quality testing
    • Collect vegetable waste for fish feed
    • Harvest ready vegetables
    • Clean holding tanks

Weekly Tasks

  • Fish System Maintenance:
    • Complete water quality analysis
    • Clean filters thoroughly
    • Check pump systems
    • Grade fish by size if needed
    • Plan harvesting schedule
  • General Tasks:
    • Deep Clean All Housing
    • Health Assessments
    • Feed Inventory
    • System Maintenance

Monthly Tasks

  • Fish Management:
    • Size grading of fish
    • Major water quality assessment
    • System deep cleaning
    • Growth rate monitoring
    • Stocking density adjustment
  • General Tasks:
    • Veterinary Checks
    • Breeding Management
    • Infrastructure Repairs
    • Supply Ordering

Seasonal Tasks

  • Fish System:
    • Major harvesting events
    • New fingerling introduction
    • System deep cleaning
    • Equipment maintenance
  • Integrated Systems:
    • Crop rotation planning
    • Feed production assessment
    • Infrastructure upgrades

Fish Processing and Storage

  • Fresh Fish Handling:
    • Clean processing area
    • Proper scaling and cleaning
    • Immediate refrigeration
    • Use within 2 days
  • Preservation Methods:
    • Smoking facility for surplus
    • Solar drying capabilities
    • Freezer storage system
    • Vacuum sealing option

Special Considerations for HIV Care

  • Fish Handling Safety:
    • Strict hygiene protocols
    • Proper cooking temperatures

    • Clean storage systems
  • Nutritional Integration:

    • Omega-3 rich selections
    • Immune-boosting combinations

  • System Maintenance:
