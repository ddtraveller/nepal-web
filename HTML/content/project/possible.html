Project Possible

Empowering Migrant Families in Nepal's Kathmandu District

Vision Statement

Project Possible aims to empower, support, and educate migrant families through three main initiatives within the Kathmandu district. We are committed to engaging with migrant families to offer targeted and responsive support, working together to create a brighter future for Nepalese children and their communities.

Understanding Nepal's Internal Migration

Internal migration in Nepal represents a complex socio-economic phenomenon driven by various factors:

Who Are the Migrants?

The migrants from Bajura and Kalikot primarily consist of farming families belonging to marginalized communities, including:

  • Traditional agricultural workers seeking better opportunities
  • Families affected by climate change and declining agricultural yields in their home regions
  • Communities historically excluded from economic development
  • Multi-generational families moving together to maintain cultural bonds

Push Factors

  • Limited agricultural opportunities in mountainous regions
  • Lack of educational infrastructure in remote areas
  • Limited access to healthcare services
  • Climate change impacts on traditional farming methods

According to the Nepal Labor Force Survey 2023/24, internal migration has increased by 15% in the past five years, with the majority of movements being from far-western regions to urban centers.

Regional Context

Initiative 1: Educational Support Program

Why It Matters

Education is crucial for breaking the cycle of poverty in migrant communities. Research shows that children of migrants face unique challenges:

  • 70% of migrant children in Kathmandu face educational disruption during transition
  • Lack of proper school supplies leads to decreased attendance and participation
  • Language barriers affect academic performance and social integration

Program Overview

Working in partnership with the Nepalese Government's funded education program, we provide essential support to ensure children can fully participate in their education journey.

Annual Resource Distribution

Supporting 100 children with essential educational materials:

  • School uniforms - ensuring equal participation and dignity
  • School shoes - enabling safe travel to school
  • Backpacks - protecting educational materials
  • Water bottles - promoting hydration and health
  • Complete stationary sets - facilitating active learning

According to the Nepal Education Report 2023, children with proper school supplies show 40% higher attendance rates and improved academic performance.

Initiative 2: Women's Health Support

Why It Matters

Women's health initiatives are crucial in migrant communities where traditional healthcare access is limited:

  • Only 24% of women in migrant communities have regular access to feminine hygiene products
  • Cervical cancer rates are 3x higher in communities without regular screening
  • Cultural barriers often prevent women from seeking preventive care

Cultural Context

In many Nepalese communities, menstruation remains a taboo subject. Traditional practices like Chhaupadi, though legally banned, still impact women's health decisions. Our program works within cultural sensitivities while promoting health and dignity.

Menstrual Health Program

Providing regular access to feminine hygiene products and education about menstrual health, reaching over 200 women monthly.

Preventive Healthcare

Annual pap smear tests for women aged 16 and above, combined with health education and awareness programs.

The Nepal Journal of Obstetrics and Gynaecology (2024) reports that regular screening programs can reduce cervical cancer mortality by up to 60% in vulnerable populations.

Initiative 3: Early Childhood Development Center

Why It Matters

Early childhood development is crucial for long-term success:

  • 90% of brain development occurs before age 5
  • Quality early education can increase lifetime earnings by 25%
  • Structured learning environments support social and cognitive development
  • Parent involvement in early education strengthens community bonds

Temporary Daycare Facility

Our facility provides:

  • Safe, nurturing environment for children aged 2-5
  • Age-appropriate learning activities based on Nepal's Early Childhood Development Framework
  • Nutritional support through daily meals
  • Parent engagement programs

English Language Program

Structured English learning sessions designed to:

  • Build foundational language skills
  • Support integration into urban educational settings
  • Enhance future educational and employment opportunities
  • Foster community connections through shared learning

The Nepal Early Childhood Development Report (2024) indicates that children who participate in quality early education programs are 40% more likely to complete secondary education.

Long-term Impact

Our integrated approach addresses immediate needs while building foundations for sustainable community development:

  • Educational support creates pathways out of poverty
  • Health initiatives improve quality of life and workforce participation
  • Early childhood programs strengthen community resilience
  • Cultural preservation alongside integration support

Based on similar programs in other regions of Nepal, integrated support initiatives show a 65% improvement in quality-of-life indicators over a five-year period.
