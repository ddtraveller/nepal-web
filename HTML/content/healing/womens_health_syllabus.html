Women's Health and Wellness Course - Syllabus

Course Information

Duration: 3 days (21 hours total)
Format: Interactive workshops and discussions
Instructors: Women's health educators, certified midwife, nutritionist, lactation consultant

Required Materials

  • Course workbook (provided)
  • Cycle tracking journal (provided)
  • Nutrition and meal planning guides (provided)
  • Comfortable clothing for movement sessions
  • Note-taking materials
  • Yoga mat for exercise sessions

Time Topic Activity Learning Objectives

9:30am - 10:30am Menstrual Cycle Physiology Interactive lecture - Identify cycle phases
- Understand hormonal changes
- Recognize normal variations
10:45am - 12:00pm Body Literacy Workshop Practical session - Learn cycle tracking methods
- Identify personal patterns
- Practice charting techniques

Course Objectives

Upon completion, participants will be able to:

  • Demonstrate understanding of menstrual cycle phases and hormonal patterns
  • Implement effective cycle tracking and body literacy practices
  • Create personalized nutrition and self-care plans
  • Understand pregnancy changes and preparation needs
  • Demonstrate basic newborn care skills
  • Apply breastfeeding techniques correctly
  • Identify when to seek professional medical care
  • Access appropriate resources and support systems

Assessment Methods

  • Participation (40%)
    • Engagement in discussions
    • Workshop participation
    • Group activities
  • Practical Skills (40%)
    • Cycle tracking practice
    • Newborn care demonstrations
    • Exercise technique execution
  • Personal Planning (20%)
    • Self-care plan development
    • Resource compilation
    • Support network identification

Course Policies

  • Attendance: Full participation recommended for maximum benefit
  • Confidentiality: All personal discussions remain private
  • Support: Individual assistance available as needed
  • Resources: Additional materials provided upon request
