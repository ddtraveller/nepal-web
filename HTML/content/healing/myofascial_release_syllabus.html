Myofascial Release and Unwinding Course - Syllabus

Course Information

Duration: 3 days (21 hours total)
Prerequisites: Current healthcare license or certification
CEU Credits: 21 hours

Required Materials

  • Course manual (provided)
  • Massage table
  • Sheets and bolsters
  • Loose, comfortable clothing
  • Note-taking materials
  • Treatment documentation forms (provided)

Time Topic Activity Learning Objectives

1:00pm - 2:30pm Upper Body Techniques Demonstration and practice Master basic release techniques

2:45pm - 4:00pm Integration Techniques Advanced practice Combine direct and indirect approaches

Detailed Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Demonstrate advanced palpation skills and listening touch
  • Perform comprehensive fascial assessment
  • Execute basic and advanced MFR techniques
  • Facilitate regional and full-body unwinding
  • Support emotional release processes
  • Create appropriate treatment plans
  • Maintain therapeutic presence during unwinding
  • Document treatment processes effectively
  • Identify and manage therapeutic boundaries
  • Recognize contraindications and precautions

Assessment Criteria

  • Practical Skills (50%)
    • Palpation accuracy
    • Technique execution
    • Body mechanics
    • Therapeutic presence
  • Clinical Reasoning (30%)
    • Assessment skills
    • Treatment planning
    • Technique selection
    • Safety awareness
  • Professional Integration (20%)
    • Documentation
    • Communication
    • Professional boundaries
    • Therapeutic relationship

Course Policies

  • Attendance: 100% attendance required for certification
  • Participation: Active engagement in all practical sessions
  • Safety: Adherence to all safety protocols
  • Boundaries: Maintenance of professional boundaries
  • Documentation: Completion of all required documentation

Continuing Education

This course provides the foundation for advanced myofascial release practice. Graduates are encouraged to:

  • Join professional MFR organizations
  • Participate in peer study groups
  • Pursue advanced training opportunities
  • Maintain detailed treatment records
  • Seek mentorship when needed
