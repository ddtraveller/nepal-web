Course Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, participants will be able to:

  • Demonstrate comprehensive understanding of craniosacral system anatomy and physiology
  • Accurately palpate and assess the craniosacral rhythm
  • Perform basic, intermediate, and advanced craniosacral techniques safely
  • Apply still point techniques and evaluate their effects
  • Work effectively with the dural membrane system
  • Understand and apply sacral decompression techniques
  • Develop appropriate treatment protocols for various conditions
  • Recognize and work with SomatoEmotional aspects of treatment
  • Identify contraindications and special considerations for CST

Assessment Criteria

  • Attendance and Participation (20%)
    • Full attendance required
    • Active participation in discussions and workshops
  • Practical Skills Assessment (60%)
    • Accurate palpation skills
    • Proper technique demonstration
    • Safe and effective application
    • Clinical reasoning
  • Clinical Integration Project (20%)
    • Case study analysis
    • Treatment planning
    • Technique application rationale

Course Policies

  • Attendance: 100% attendance is required for certification
  • Practical Sessions: Students must participate in all hands-on activities
  • Professional Conduct: Professional behavior and appropriate attire required
  • Safety: All techniques must be practiced within safety guidelines
  • Respect: Maintain appropriate professional boundaries during practice sessions

Note: This foundational certification in Craniosacral Therapy represents Level 1 training. Advanced certification requires completion of additional coursework and documented practice hours. Students are encouraged to join professional CST organizations for continuing education opportunities.
