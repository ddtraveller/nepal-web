Practical Life Skills for Nepali Women - Syllabus

Course Information

Duration: 3 days (21 hours total)
Language: Nepali (English support available)
Location: Community center with kitchen and sewing facilities

Required Materials

  • Course Workbook (provided)
  • Sewing Kit:
    • Basic scissors
    • Measuring tape
    • Needles and threads
    • Practice fabrics
  • Cooking Materials:
    • Apron
    • Food containers
    • Recipe notebook
  • Personal Items:
    • Notebook and pen
    • Water bottle
    • Hair covering for cooking

Safety Guidelines

  • Proper handling of sewing equipment
  • Kitchen safety protocols
  • Food hygiene standards
  • Safe use of cleaning materials

Day 1: Sewing and Textile Skills

Time Topic Activity Learning Objectives
9:00am - 9:30am Course Introduction Welcome and orientation - Understand course structure
- Review safety guidelines
- Meet fellow participants
9:30am - 10:30am Hand Sewing Basics Demonstration and practice - Learn basic stitches
- Threading techniques
- Knot methods
10:45am - 12:00pm Machine Sewing Hands-on workshop - Machine operation
- Basic maintenance
- Practice stitching
1:00pm - 2:30pm Garment Repair Practical session - Patch application
- Button replacement
- Zipper repair
2:45pm - 4:00pm Simple Garment Creation Project work - Pattern understanding
- Cutting techniques
- Basic construction

Day 2: Cooking and Food Preservation

Time Topic Activity Learning Objectives
9:00am - 10:30am Traditional Cooking Cooking workshop - Dal bhat preparation
- Spice blending
- Cooking techniques
10:45am - 12:00pm Food Preservation Hands-on practice - Pickle making
- Achar preparation
- Storage methods
1:00pm - 2:30pm Festival Foods Demonstration and practice - Sel roti making
- Sweet preparations
- Festival specialties
2:45pm - 4:00pm Kitchen Management Organization workshop - Ingredient storage
- Meal planning
- Budget cooking

Day 3: Home Management and Life Skills

Time Topic Activity Learning Objectives
9:00am - 10:30am Natural Cleaning Practical workshop - Natural cleaners
- Traditional methods
- Eco-friendly practices
10:45am - 12:00pm Home Organization Interactive session - Space optimization
- Storage solutions
- Daily routines
1:00pm - 2:30pm Traditional Remedies Demonstration and practice - Herbal preparations
- Common remedies
- Safe usage guidelines
2:45pm - 4:00pm Modern Solutions Integration workshop - Time management
- Resource optimization
- Problem-solving skills

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, participants will be able to:

  • Perform basic hand and machine sewing
  • Complete common garment repairs
  • Create simple garments from patterns
  • Prepare traditional Nepali dishes
  • Make and preserve pickles and achars
  • Cook festival specialties
  • Implement efficient home organization
  • Use traditional cleaning methods
  • Prepare basic home remedies
  • Apply modern household solutions

Assessment Methods

  • Practical Skills (60%)
    • Sewing project completion
    • Cooking demonstrations
    • Home management tasks
  • Participation (20%)
    • Workshop engagement
    • Group activities
    • Skill practice
  • Knowledge Integration (20%)
    • Recipe collection
    • Resource compilation
    • Practical applications

Course Policies

  • Attendance: Full participation recommended
  • Safety: Must follow all safety guidelines
  • Materials: Proper care of provided equipment
  • Community: Respectful participation required

Additional Resources

Participants receive:

  • Recipe collection handbook
  • Pattern templates
  • Resource guide
  • Supplier contacts
  • Community group connections
  • Monthly skill-sharing meetup invitations
